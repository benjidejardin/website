About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Economics specializing in labor economics. My research focuses on understanding labor market dynamics, wage inequality, and the impact of policy interventions on employment outcomes.

Through rigorous empirical analysis and innovative methodological approaches, I aim to contribute to our understanding of how workers and firms interact in modern labor markets, with particular attention to questions of equity and efficiency.

Work in Progress

Labor Market Dynamics and Wage Inequality

Status: In Progress

This project examines the relationship between labor market concentration and wage dispersion. Using administrative data, I analyze how employer market power affects earnings across different skill levels and industries.

The Impact of Minimum Wage Policies on Employment

Status: Data Collection

This study investigates the employment effects of minimum wage increases using quasi-experimental methods. I focus on heterogeneous effects across demographic groups and local labor market conditions.

Remote Work and Labor Market Outcomes

Status: Early Stage

This research explores how the shift to remote work has affected job mobility, wage growth, and geographic labor market integration. I use novel data to understand the long-term implications for workers and employers.
